# Product Vision

## Product Name

Finance Audit Management (`FAM`)

## Vision Statement

Finance Audit Management is an AI-assisted audit response management platform that turns unstructured Individual Document Requests (`IDRs`) into structured, evidence-backed, reviewable, and submission-ready response packages. The product combines document understanding, response strategy generation, task orchestration, evidence collection, human review, and package assembly in a governed operating model designed for audit defensibility.

## Product Positioning

FAM must be positioned as an enterprise audit workbench and system of audit record. It is not a simple chatbot, prompt wrapper, or generic retrieval application. Conversational assistance may exist as a secondary interaction pattern, but the core product is a case-centric workflow platform that manages questions, tasks, evidence, approvals, and submissions.

## Vision Outcomes

- Reduce manual effort spent interpreting IDRs, locating precedent, coordinating stakeholders, and assembling response packages.
- Improve consistency and defensibility of audit responses through structured strategy, evidence validation, and controlled approvals.
- Create a reusable semantic and operational foundation across audits, jurisdictions, entities, and request types.
- Preserve end-to-end lineage from source request through final submitted package.
- Establish a scalable foundation for enterprise audit intelligence beyond the first pilot.

## Core Experience

The core user experience is an audit workbench with:

- case intake and request understanding
- question-level strategy and risk framing
- dependency-aware task orchestration
- evidence intake, validation, and lineage
- reviewer consoles and approval gates
- final submission package assembly

## Product Principles

- Case-centric, not chat-centric
- Strategy before drafting
- Human approval gates are mandatory
- Evidence lineage is a first-class requirement
- Retrieval is permission-aware and audit-scoped
- Learning improves prompts, templates, ontology, and workflows under governance

## Success Definition

FAM succeeds when audit teams can take an incoming IDR, understand it faster, coordinate the right work across teams, ground responses in accepted evidence, and produce a final package with stronger traceability and lower execution risk than today’s fragmented manual process.
